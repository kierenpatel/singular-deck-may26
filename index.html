04 · Worldview · the counter-proposal

Small models win — data, clock, economics.

Foundation models fit unstructured data at internet scale. Singular's stack — paired spatial + clinical, per-marker FDA gates, per-call economics — rewards small supervised models.

01 · DATA STRUCTURE

Our data is paired, not unlabeled.

Self-supervision needs billions of unlabeled images. We have paired (spatial → IHC → outcome) triples. Direct supervision is faster, cheaper, more accurate per marker — 200–500 cases per marker.

02 · REGULATORY GATES

FDA clears per-indication. Models should match.

SaMD requires clearance per use case. Per-marker supervised = 1:1 with the FDA submission. Foundation-model path validates the base AND every downstream use — additive cost, not substitutive.

03 · PER-CALL ECONOMICS

10–50× cheaper inference per call.

Small supervised (50–100M params): ~$0.005–0.02 / call. ViT-L foundation (600M+): ~$0.10–0.40 / call. At pharma's $5–15/call, this is the difference between 95%+ GM and 80% GM. Compounds across 200K+ calls/yr.

04 · UPDATE CADENCE

10–14 weeks per new marker.

New ADC target every ~3 months across 200+ programs. Small model: 10–14 weeks · $200–400K. Foundation-model retrain: 6–12 months · $5M+. Slower than the market.

05 · INTERPRETABILITY

Auditable predictions are non-negotiable.

FDA, payors, pathologists demand it. Small supervised: SHAP / Grad-CAM localize which cells drove the call. Foundation attention maps are diffuse — hard to defend in 510(k) or peer review.

06 · FAILURE ISOLATION

One marker breaks → only that one retrains.

Drift detection on a single classifier is cheap and fast. Foundation-model regression cascades through every downstream use simultaneously. Operational risk is asymmetric.

Bottom line: small ≠ inferior. Small means the architecture matches the buyer, regulator, clock, and margin. Foundation models solve a different problem for a different customer.

FOUR PROBLEMS WITH CURRENT IHC SCORING
01 · Binary in a continuous world
0/1+/2+/3+ buckets ignore the actual antigen density gradient. Enhertu's HER2-low patients were called "negative" by old IHC.
02 · Spatial heterogeneity invisible
Pathologist gives one whole-slide score. Tumors aren't uniform — heterogeneity drives recurrence patterns ADCs can't reach.

Two pathologists scoring the same slide give different answers ~1 in 3 times. Trial enrollment depends on this coin-flip.
04 · No bystander-zone modeling
